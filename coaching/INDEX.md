## Principles Learned

1. **Central pawns need piece support** — d4+c4 is strong when followed by Nc3, Nf3, Bf4. Pushing e4/f4 too early without development = overextension.
2. **Know the traps in your opening move order** — 2.c4 after 1.d4 is almost always correct, but know the Budapest (2...e5) and Albin (after 1...d5 2.c4 e5).
3. **Simplify the toolchain** — if a tool's role is fully replaced by another, remove it from the mandatory workflow. Keep it as optional.
